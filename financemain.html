Preparation for analysis

Our dataset provides 200+ indicators, however, most of them are derived from each other and are not suitable for our analysis. So, the first step is to choose right indicator to create the portfolio. The given ratios have been chosen for the analysis:

1. Cash Ratio –  It is a liquidity measure and helps firms to cover short-term debts. Cash Ratio=Cash/Current liabilities

2. Return on Equity – Return on equity is one of the unique measures to see the profitability of the company. 
Return on Equity=Net income/ Shareholder’s Equity

3. Dividend Yield – Dividend yield means which percentage of the price of each share is returned by cash to the investor.
Dividend Yield=Dividend per share/Price per share

4. Price Earnings ratio – PER indicates how fast companies develop and usually higher PER is preferred by investors.
  Price Earnings ratio=Price per share/Earnings per share

5. Asset Turnover -  ATR is one of the main ratios to take into account while evaluating stocks.
  Asset Turnover=Total sales/Assets

6. Debt Ratio – High debt ratio means that the company has the capital to invest in its future and it means a higher debt ratio is better. Debt Ratio=Total liability /Assets
